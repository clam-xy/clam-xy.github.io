In this part we look at our network from a spatial perspective. We have 19 different regions : ‘Korean’, ‘Catholicandtheologians’, ‘Finnish’, ‘Slovene’, ‘Italian’, ‘French’, ‘Chinese’, ‘ancientGreek’, ‘Muslim’, ‘Russian’, ‘Japanese’, ‘Canadian’, ‘Romanian’, ‘Indian’, ‘German-language’, ‘Iranian’, ‘American’, ‘peoplefromtheBasqueCountry’, ‘British’.

We will try to characterize as much as possible each of these regions and see if we can highlight some patterns.

Connections inside and outside each era :

Do we have some specifities for each region ? Are some of them more ‘philosophical’ ?

As for the eras and the cores ares we will look at the inside and outside edges. For one region are there more connections inside this era or outside ?

  • Firstly the number of philosophers per region tell us about the contribution of some regions in philosophy. Most countries with a significant number of philosophers come from Europe. We find that France, Germany, England, Italy and Catholican (linked to the Vatican) have a majority of philosophers. We also find more philosophers from ancient Greece, America and Japan.

  • We note that it is among these important countries that we find the most internal connections. France, Ancient Greece and Germany are very dense sub-graphs compared to the other regions.

For the comparison between internal and external links we can see two types of results.

  • Firstly, among the countries with many philosophers. Several of these countries such as France, Italy and America have an equal and not disproportionate proportion of links going outwards and links coming inwards. Despite the fact that these countries have a substantial number of philosophers, they do not have many links to other regions. For Germany and England, however, there is a high proportion of external links. This allows us to highlight, between close geographical areas, regions that are more affluent or more ‘international’ than others. Here the philosophies of Germany and England have probably had more influence on different countries.

  • Among the countries with fewer philosophers we find the same observation but with even more contrasting results. For Slovenia and Russia we have a higher number of links coming from outside (in proportion) whereas for the Muslims it is the opposite. We can see that even if a region has few philosophers initially, some of them have had an important influence or have contributed to the work of other philosophers around the world.

The philosophy focuses on a few main geographical areas, but this study shows that there is a fairly well-connected global network that is not focused on any one country.

The most linked eras :

As we have links between philosophers from different regions we can create a graph of these regions by looking at which ones are most connected. The darker the red on the graph the more important the link. As we have a lot of regions we focused on the most important ones :

Germany takes a leading position in this graph between England, France and America which forms the main cluster. Japan is quite far away. Surprisingly, Italy is quite far from the centre of the graph despite its geographical position close to France and England. We therefore find what we saw before with Germany and England which are quite ‘international’ and form connections with philosophers from all over the world.

Bag of words analysis :

It could be difficult to study the philosophical themes and issues for each region. In fact we will find surely some specific famous persons for each of these regions.The aim is therefore to be able to detect among these people whether they come from a common background and whether this background can indicate a pattern for the region under study.

  • Europe :

It can therefore be seen that most of the country-specific words correspond to well-known personalities. For England, Italy and Germany. For France we find a majority of writers and novelists. Several artists are present in the bag of words for Finland while for Russia we find the important leaders of the Communist regime. Only for Ancient Greece do we find the themes found in the timeline studies. These bag of words show how philosophy can touch many fields.

  • Asia :

In this region we see a great deal of influence from one region to another. In Japan we have Mao and Tsongkhapa and in Korea we have Confucian and Confucius. In addition we find a strong Buddhist influence in Japan. China shows a particular bag of words. Indeed we find quite scientific terms marking a certain evolution of philosophy: starry, retinoblastoma or Guoping who is a Chinese neuroscientist.

  • America :

We see a strong political influence in the US with Addams, Jefferson and Emerson. As in China we see the emergence of very scientific issues in Canada: realism, neurophilosophy and hacking.

We have not filled in all the bag of words for each region here. As you may have noticed each region is very specific we can detect some characteristics and links between them but each region would deserve a specific study
